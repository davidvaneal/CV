About

I am a professional in the field of Biology from the University of Quindío in Armenia, Colombia. My fascination with insects, especially dung beetles, has led me to delve deeper into the field of entomology, taxonomy, and ecology. Currently, I am expanding my knowledge and skills at the Institute of Ecology - INECOL in Xalapa, Mexico, where I am pursuing a master’s degree.

My enthusiasm for life is manifested in my dedication to research, which allows me to explore and understand the complex processes of nature, thus connecting with the essence of reality. The mountain is my sanctuary; practicing trekking at high altitudes offers me a space for introspection and appreciation of the intrinsic value of life.

I firmly believe in the importance of “sharing” as a pillar of human growth. Therefore, I strive daily to enrich this aspect of my life, always seeking to contribute significantly to the conservation of our natural environment and the well-being of society.
